01

Three Scenarios · Genuinely Different

Best · Base · Oh-No — side by side

Build all three as separate columns with assumptions that actually diverge. The Oh-No should make you uncomfortable — if it doesn't, you haven't built it honestly. For each scenario, capture the assumptions that drive it, the three coverage questions, and the three leading indicators that signal you're tracking toward that outcome.

02

The Oh-No Response Plan

Three categories · pre-decided · calm

Your Oh-No requires a pre-decided response plan — not a reaction. Write it now, while calm. When the trigger arrives, you're executing, not inventing. Three categories, in the order you'd pull them.

If sales are consistently 40–50% slower than base case for 4–5 months in a row — which is not worst case, just the pattern of consecutive underperformance that catches founders off guard — here is what we do.

The Honest Question

Based on evidence · not hope

Based on what you know today — actual pipeline, conversion rates so far, sales cycle experience, momentum in your business development — which scenario does the evidence point toward? Not which one you want. Which one the data suggests. This determines where your energy goes right now.
